Kibbeh Morsels

Description

This recipe is for a traditional levantine dish comprised of fried burgul morsels with lamb meat fillings.

Ingredients

The Filling

The filling is comprised of pan-fried chunks of ground lamb meat mixed with caramelized white onions. This creates a rich mix between the sweet and savory flavors.

The Spices

You can find ready-mixed kibbeh spices at any levantine food store, but it is always better to make your own. You have more control on the flavors this way.

For a proper traditional seven spice mix we'll need:

  • 5 tbsp garam masala or allspice
  • 2 tbsp turmeric
  • 2 tbsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp coriander
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp cayenne pepper

The Meat

For best results, we use a mix of lamb and beef mince. This ensures our meat is not too fatty, but also tender enough. The fattier lamb mince will also allow us to form the meat better.I like to use a 50:50 mix, but feel free to experiment to your heart's delight!

Instructions

  1. Mix 2 lb of lamb and beef mince in 50:50 proportion and divide into two equal portions
  2. Chop 1 head of onion into a medium dice
  3. Mix 1 lb of the mince and onions, and pan fry on medium heat until golden brown
  4. Mix 1 lb of fine burgul with 1 lb of the mince and form small circlets
  5. Fill each ciclet with a generous amount of our pan-fried meat and form into a closed elipse shaped dumpling
  6. Fry dumplings in oil until golden brown in color
  7. Serve with a garlic-yoghurt mix
